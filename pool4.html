The Game of Skill and Strategy

ABOUT POOL

Pool, also known as pocket billiards, is a fascinating cue sport that combines physics, geometry, and psychology. Played on a rectangular table with six pockets, players use a cue stick to strike cue balls against object balls.

With origins dating back to the 15th century, pool has evolved into a popular recreational activity and competitive sport enjoyed by millions worldwide in homes, bars, and professional tournaments.

Beyond being just a game, pool teaches patience, concentration, and strategic thinking while providing social interaction and friendly competition.

DESCRIBING POPULAR GAMES

8-BALL

The most popular pool game worldwide. Players are assigned either stripes or solids and must pocket all of their group before legally pocketing the 8-ball to win.

9-BALL

Players must hit the lowest-numbered ball first. The game is won by legally pocketing the 9-ball. Known for its fast-paced action and combination shots.

Straight Pool (14.1 Continuous)

Also called 14.1 continuous. Players call any ball and pocket. The first player to reach a predetermined score wins. Favored by many professionals.

POOL TECHNIQUES

Mastering the Basics

A solid, balanced stance is the foundation of a good shot. Keep your feet shoulder-width apart, with your front foot slightly forward. Bend at the waist until your chin is directly above the cue.

Your bridge hand provides stability for the cue. The closed bridge offers more control for powerful shots, while the open bridge is great for finesse shots and English.

Applying side spin to the cue ball allows you to control its path after contact. This advanced technique is essential for position play and making difficult shots.
